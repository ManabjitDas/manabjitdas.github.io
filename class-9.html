Members: Sushmita, Udangsri, Sarika, Priyanka, and Shrine have worked together on the captivating topic of holograms, a cutting-edge technology that creates three-dimensional images or projections. Their project explores the science behind holography, which uses light interference patterns to create realistic 3D images that appear to float in space. The team’s website explains the various types of holograms, from simple static images to complex interactive projections, and their applications in fields such as entertainment, medical imaging, and data visualization. They highlight how holograms are revolutionizing industries by offering immersive experiences and innovative ways to display information. Through their project, the team aims to show how holography is pushing the boundaries of technology, enabling futuristic visual displays that could change the way we communicate, learn, and experience the world.

Members:Nishita, Jupitara, Mousumi, Mayuri, and Arish have focused on the topic of map indicators, which are essential tools for navigation and location identification. Their project explains how map indicators, such as symbols, legends, and scales, help users understand geographical information and accurately interpret maps. The team’s website explores the different types of map indicators, including directional indicators, scale bars, and color codes, and how they provide context to features like roads, landmarks, and elevation. They also highlight the importance of accurate map indicators in various fields, from urban planning and travel to environmental studies and disaster management. Through their work, they emphasize how these tools make maps more accessible and useful, ensuring that users can easily find their way and comprehend spatial data. The project demonstrates the crucial role of map indicators in modern navigation systems.

Members:Kritiman, Prince, Deepjit, Dhrubo, and Piyom have worked together on the topic of the water level indicator, a crucial device used to monitor and manage the water levels in tanks, reservoirs, or other storage systems. Their project explains how this device helps prevent overflow or dry running by providing real-time information about the water levels. The team’s website showcases the working principle behind the water level indicator, which uses sensors to detect the water level and sends a signal to a display or alarm when it reaches a certain point. They also highlight its applications in domestic, industrial, and agricultural settings, ensuring efficient water usage and preventing wastage. Through their project, they demonstrate how technology can optimize water management, playing a key role in conservation and reducing water scarcity issues.

Members: Lalit, Jeet, Roni, Siddharth, and Shubham have collaborated on the topic of the electrolysis of water, a fascinating scientific process in which water (H₂O) is split into hydrogen (H₂) and oxygen (O₂) through the application of an electric current. Their project explores the principles of electrolysis, demonstrating how water can be decomposed into its constituent gases by passing an electric current through it. The team’s website explains the key components of the electrolysis setup, including the electrodes, the electrolyte, and the electrical energy required for the process. It highlights the significance of water electrolysis in renewable energy production, particularly in hydrogen fuel cells, which have the potential to provide a clean and sustainable energy source. Their work not only educates about the process itself but also emphasizes its importance in advancing green technologies and the future of energy.

Members: Debajit, Nayan, Piyush, Shubh, and Binit have worked on the topic of the advanced railway bridge, a modern engineering marvel designed to improve the safety, efficiency, and sustainability of railway transportation. Their project explores the latest innovations in bridge design, materials, and construction techniques that allow for longer-lasting, more durable structures capable of supporting high-speed trains and heavy cargo. The team’s website delves into the key features of advanced railway bridges, such as reinforced concrete, steel composite structures, and innovative load-bearing designs. They also focus on the integration of smart technologies, such as sensors for real-time monitoring of bridge health, to prevent accidents and improve maintenance. The project highlights how these modern bridges are crucial in supporting the growing demands of rail networks, ensuring smooth, safe, and efficient transport systems for the future.

Members: Sahul, Tobias, Danel, Sayan The Newton disc demonstrates the concept of color mixing through rapid rotation. By spinning a disc with segments painted in different colors, the human eye perceives it as white due to the blending of colors. This experiment illustrates how primary colors combine to form white light, supporting Newton’s theory of light and color. The group will showcase the disc's rotation, explain the science behind color perception, and highlight its relevance in understanding light behavior.

Members: Gargi, Moromi, Rehuma, Mafiza, and Rina are working on a project focused on the Earthquake Bell, a device that detects seismic activity and issues an early warning to save lives during earthquakes. The Earthquake Bell operates by sensing ground vibrations caused by seismic waves and triggering an alert, allowing people time to take safety precautions before the shaking reaches them. This device has a crucial role in disaster preparedness, especially in earthquake-prone areas. The team has worked together to design and develop a website that explains the technology behind the Earthquake Bell, how it functions, and its importance in early warning systems. Through their work, they aim to highlight the significance of such innovations in minimizing damage and preventing loss of life during natural disasters.

Members: Arif, Sayed, Mustafizur, Ranbir, and Zuned have teamed up to present the fascinating topic of radar and the solar system. Their project explores the role of radar technology in studying celestial bodies, allowing scientists to gather critical data about planets, moons, and other objects in the solar system. By emitting radio waves, radar systems can detect and measure distances, even through clouds or dust, making it invaluable for space exploration. The team’s website delves into how radar helps in mapping planetary surfaces, monitoring atmospheric conditions, and discovering new celestial phenomena. Their work emphasizes how radar has become an essential tool for deepening our understanding of the solar system, highlighting its role in missions to planets like Mars and beyond. The project showcases the impact of technology on space exploration and its potential for uncovering the mysteries of the universe.

Their model will include solar-powered streetlights, buildings, and transportation systems, emphasizing the importance of renewable energy in combating climate change and ensuring a greener future.

Members: Abhijeet, Anshuman, Ayan, Arnab, Manik, Rayhan The anti-sleep alarm is designed to prevent accidents caused by drowsiness, especially among drivers. This device detects signs of sleepiness, such as head nodding or prolonged eye closure, and triggers an alert to keep the user awake. The group will demonstrate how their model uses sensors to monitor fatigue and emit sound or vibration warnings. Their project emphasizes the importance of staying alert, particularly during long drives, and highlights the role of technology in enhancing road safety and reducing accidents.

Members: Lakshita, Enamoni, Jyoti, Prachi This group will demonstrate the effectiveness of hand sanitizers in killing germs and preventing the spread of infections. Their experiment will compare different sanitizer brands and show how alcohol-based solutions eliminate bacteria and viruses on contact. Using practical tests, they will highlight the importance of proper hand hygiene in maintaining health, especially during pandemics. The exhibit will also explain how sanitizers work, emphasizing their role in reducing the transmission of diseases in daily life.

Members: Priyanshu, Biplab, Ranak, Ayushman, Piyush The obstacle avoidance car uses sensors and advanced algorithms to navigate without colliding into objects. This model features ultrasonic sensors that detect obstacles in the car’s path, triggering automatic steering adjustments to avoid them. The group will demonstrate how this technology can be applied in autonomous vehicles, improving safety and reducing human error. Their exhibit will showcase how smart navigation systems are shaping the future of transportation by making cars more intelligent and safer for drivers and pedestrians alike.
